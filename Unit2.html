2.1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright and other intellectual property laws affect websites as it may restrict what can be published. Unfortunately these days copyright law is breached in many places over the internet.
The best way to get around this is first, get permission from the original author, second, if you want to publish information on your site from another site, link to it and perhaps get permission to publish an excerpt from the original site.

Copyright protects works such as:
Literature - articles, computer software, smartphone apps, essays, poems, novels.
Music - lyrics, musical notes, melodies, operas, musical plays.
Sound recordings - recorded speeches, cd, podcasts etc.
Audio-visual - television shows, movies, online videos , video games, slideshows.
Art - photographs, graphics, sculptures, maps, diagrams, fine arts.
Architectural - plans and drawings associated with it.

2.1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

There are people with special needs in terms of getting access to the internet and websites. One group are people with sight problems such as colour blinds, where high contrast, large, simple fonts are extremely important to help them surfing over the web. Talking web pages and text-to-speech are also very useful.
Other group are people with hearing problems where i.e. subtitles in movies ore the only way to understand the content. No one should be excluded from access to the internet resources and websites, so this is a great opportunity for IT community to help people without it
