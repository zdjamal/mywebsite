1.1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

Information Technology (IT) plays a very important and integral role in most aspects of life especially in the business sector. Indeed modern businesses of all types and sizes rely in one way or another on IT to conduct their business and achieve their goals. IT enables modern businesses to grow quicker, deliver a better customer experience and save time and money.

Technologies such as artificial intelligence, machine learning and cloud computing are essential for modern businesses to understand their target market faster and more efficiently. Small businesses can survive and compete with big companies due to the advent of the internet and IT.

Emails, Voice Over Internet Protocol(VOIP), text, video and audio chats are means of communication between employees, customers and suppliers and a must have technologies for modern businesses.

IT systems such as Inventory Management Systems (IMS), Data Management Systems (DMS), Management Information Systems (MIS) and many more are very useful and important for all types of modern businesses to track the quality and availability of each item and update information. DMS enables businesses to store and manage a huge amount of data on servers and storage devices which can be available and accessible by everyone in the company regardless of their geographical locations. IT also enables companies managers to analyse and make decisions based on information and analysis provided by a MIS. By using data analysis, a system such as Customer Relationship Management (CEM) can be used to manage and improve a business’s relationship with current or potential customers.

More than ever, modern businesses are subjects to security threats and breaches to financial data, customer information and other information that may cause competitive advantages. IT plays a big role in protecting businesses against security threats and vandalism by using tools such as password protection, prevention and detection systems.

Customer Relationship Management:
CRM is an approach to manage a company’s relationship with current or potential customer. It uses data analysis about customer’s history with a company to improve business relationship with customers, specifically focusing on customer retention and ultimately driving sales growth.

Communication and some IT skills as well as the willingness to learn and improve are needed to complete digital projects successfully. Splitting jobs between team members taking into consideration each member's ability to assign each part of the project to the right person are some of the methods that can be used to ensure a digital project success. Finally, planning, making, managing time, team working, error checking and testing are all part of a successful digital project completion.

1.1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a regulation in EU law on data protection and privacy for all citizens of the European Union (EU) and the European Economic Area (EEA).

GDPR aims primarily to give control to European citizens to control over their personal data and to simplify law for international businesses by unifying the regulations within the EU.

Under this regulation no one should use other individual or company personal data or intellectual property without consent.
